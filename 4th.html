Section 4: Cyclical Journey of Life
Many Eastern philosophies, such as Hinduism, Buddhism, and Taoism, see life as a continuous cycle of birth, life, death, and rebirth — rather than a straight path. This cycle, called samsara, is influenced by karma, where actions in this life shape the conditions of the next. The ultimate goal is liberation — nirvana or moksha — freedom from the cycle.
  • Samsara: The endless cycle of birth, life, death, and rebirth.
  • Karma: The law of cause and effect — good actions bring positive results, bad actions bring negative ones.
  • Nirvana/Moksha: Liberation from the cycle of rebirth.
  • Life’s journey is a process of growth, learning, and spiritual progress.
  • The cycle is often compared to the seasons: spring (birth), summer (growth), autumn (aging), and winter (death), followed by renewal.

Section 3: How Eastern Philosophies Emphasize Teamwork, Family Obligations, and Social Harmony
Eastern philosophies see individuals as part of a larger community. They emphasize harmony with others, caring for family, and supporting the well-being of society as a path to personal fulfillment. This contrasts with many Western philosophies, which often focus more on personal freedom and independence.
  • Confucianism centers on fulfilling one’s role in society — as a good child, loyal partner, or just leader — to maintain social order and peace.
  • Buddhism and Mahayana teach that all beings are interconnected, and the idea of a separate self is an illusion; they encourage compassion and selfless help for others.
  • Taoism emphasizes living in harmony with the Tao (the natural flow of life), valuing balance, flexibility, and allowing things to happen naturally without force.
